Why repurpose your blog to social?

  • Faster distribution: The hard part (research & writing) is done. Repurposing turns one article into 5–10 social assets.
  • Audience fit: LinkedIn wants scannable lines; X wants punch; Instagram wants visual + a concise caption.
  • Compounding reach: Each platform highlights a different angle from the same article—more surface area, more opportunities to engage.

Extracting high-signal snippets

Open your blog and mark:

  1. Hook-worthy line(s): a bold claim, counterintuitive insight, or crisp benefit.
  2. 3–7 key points: stats, steps, examples, quotes.
  3. 1 CTA: read full post, try product, get template, join list.
  4. 1 proof: metric, case, or testimonial to anchor credibility.

These become the building blocks for each platform template below.

7-step repurposing workflow

  1. Pick one blog: preferably evergreen.
  2. Highlight 3–7 snippets: hooks, stats, tips.
  3. Choose a single CTA: avoid splitting attention.
  4. Draft per-platform: use the templates above.
  5. Add visuals: a quote card, chart, or cover image boosts saves.
  6. Schedule smartly: stagger across 5–7 days, not all at once.
  7. Measure & iterate: watch saves, clicks, replies—then double down on what resonates.

How many posts per blog?

  • Baseline: 1 LinkedIn post, 1 IG caption, 1 email snippet, 1 X thread.
  • Stretch goal: +1 follow-up on LinkedIn (a mini-case), +1 single-tweet variant, +1 IG carousel summary.
  • Long tail: Revisit the same blog 30–60 days later with a new angle or stat update.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Copy/paste duplication: adapt structure and voice for each platform.
  • Too many CTAs: keep it to one action per post.
  • Hashtag overload: 3–5 relevant tags beat 30 generic ones.
  • Burying the hook: start with your strongest line.
