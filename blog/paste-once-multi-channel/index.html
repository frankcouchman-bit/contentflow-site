Who this is for (and not for)

This workflow is for solo creators, small teams, and founder-led startups who already produce some original content (blog posts, emails, podcast notes, research) but struggle to keep up with the mechanics of posting consistently across channels. If you want to repurpose without sounding robotic and keep your examples/data intact, you’re in the right place.

If you’re looking for one-click firehose content with zero editing, that’s not us. We optimize for quality, brand voice, and fast iteration—not spam.

Step 1 — Prepare your source

Great repurposing starts with a tight source. You need: a single claim, one example, and a simple CTA. Keep it factual and conversational. Trim anything that doesn’t serve the point.

Good source (example):

“We cut onboarding churn by 23% in 60 days by replacing a 7-field signup with a 2-step flow. The unlock was sending a usage-based welcome email on day 1. Here’s the flow and the email.”

Step 2 — Generate JSON-clean drafts (why structure matters)

ContentRepurpose returns a clean JSON object so you can copy/paste without fighting formatting: 

{
  "linkedin": "6–10 lines with a clear hook and CTA...",
  "x": ["<=280 char variant 1", "<=280 char variant 2"],
  "email": { "subject": "Short subject", "body": "3–5 sentence body" },
  "instagram": "Caption + 3–5 hashtags"
}

This structure powers length checks (≤280 on X), line counts on LinkedIn, and IG hashtag hints. It also ensures our Notion/Trello exports are readable out-of-the-box.

System prompt we use (simplified)

Return pure JSON only with keys: linkedin, x, email, instagram.
- linkedin: 6–10 lines, one CTA.
- x: <= 280 chars total; provide an array with 2 short variants.
- email: object {subject, body} — 3–5 sentences.
- instagram: 1–2 short paragraphs + 3–5 hashtags.
STRICT: No Markdown, no triple backticks, no prose — JSON object only.

Step 3 — Edit for voice & proof (2–3 minutes)

  • Add one number. % change, time saved, or cost reduced.
  • Name the noun. “We reduced onboarding steps from 7 to 2” is stronger than “we simplified onboarding.”
  • Keep hooks honest. Avoid over-promise; people smell it a mile away.
  • One CTA. Comment, DM, link, or “reply ‘template’ and I’ll send it.” Don’t stack three asks.

Channel guardrails you can steal

  • LinkedIn: 6–10 lines, line breaks after 1–2 sentences, 1 CTA, no walls of text.
  • X (Twitter): ≤280 characters (our counter helps), two variants to A/B manually.
  • Email: subject ≤55 chars; 3–5 sentences. Put the “why now” in sentence one.
  • Instagram: 1–2 punchy paragraphs, 3–5 relevant hashtags, avoid hashtag stuffing.

Step 4 — Export to Notion & Trello (one click)

Exports are opinionated so your team can move without reformatting. Notion creates a page with your LinkedIn/X/Email/IG blocks separated by headings. Trello makes a card using your channel title + body in the description.

Tip: Use Trello labels for “Channel: LinkedIn/X/Email/IG” and lists for “Draft → Ready → Scheduled.”

Step 5 — Publish & measure (simple beats perfect)

Our goal is weekly consistency. Start with a single input every Monday. Repurpose, export, assign, and ship by Thursday. If you’re on Creator+, connect Buffer to queue directly. Otherwise, the “Open Composer” buttons get you 90% of the way.

  • LinkedIn: CTR on “See more,” comments, profile visits.
  • X: Profile clicks, replies, bookmarks.
  • Email: Opens are noisy—watch replies and CTR.
  • IG: Saves > likes. Track saves/comments.

Common mistakes (and quick fixes)

  • Wall of text on LinkedIn. Fix: line breaks every 1–2 sentences; 6–10 lines total.
  • Overstuffed IG hashtags. Fix: 3–5 specific tags; remove generic spammy ones.
  • Vague CTAs. Fix: “Reply ‘template’” or “DM ‘flow’”—one verb, one action.
  • Rewriting the source each time. Fix: reuse the same paragraph across channels; only edit examples/tone.
